08. Applications III: Geometric Problems

Date: 2025-12-09 · Duration: 90 min · Tags: applications, geometry

Overview: MVEE, Chebyshev center, best-fit shapes, distance problems.

Prerequisites: Lecture 03

Learning Objectives

After this lecture, you should understand:

  • How to formulate and solve problems involving distances between sets.
  • The formulation of the minimum volume enclosing ellipsoid (MVEE) problem.
  • How to formulate and solve problems involving Chebyshev centers.

Key Concepts

1. Distance Between Convex Sets

For convex sets $\mathcal{C}_1, \mathcal{C}_2$, the distance $d(\mathcal{C}_1, \mathcal{C}_2) = \min_{x_1 \in \mathcal{C}_1, x_2 \in \mathcal{C}_2} \|x_1 - x_2\|_2$ is a convex optimization problem. The dual formulation involves separating hyperplanes, and the solution provides the closest pair of points.

2. Euclidean Projection onto Convex Sets

Projection of point $x_0$ onto convex set $\mathcal{C}$ solves $\min_{x \in \mathcal{C}} \|x - x_0\|_2^2$, a strongly convex QP with unique solution. The projection operator is non-expansive and forms the basis for projection algorithms (POCS, Dykstra's algorithm).

3. Minimum Volume Enclosing Ellipsoid (MVEE)

The MVEE for points $x_1, \ldots, x_m$ minimizes $\log \det(A^{-1})$ subject to $\|A^{1/2}(x_i - x_c)\|_2 \leq 1$ for all $i$. This is a convex problem (determinant maximization) solved via barrier methods, providing optimal ellipsoidal approximation.

4. Chebyshev Center and Maximum Inscribed Ball

The Chebyshev center of polyhedron $\{x : a_i^\top x \leq b_i\}$ maximizes radius $r$ subject to $a_i^\top x_c + r\|a_i\|_2 \leq b_i$ for all $i$. This LP finds the largest ball fitting inside the set, useful for robustness analysis and center estimation.

5. Separating and Supporting Hyperplanes

For disjoint convex sets, a separating hyperplane exists: $a^\top x_1 < b < a^\top x_2$ for $x_1 \in \mathcal{C}_1, x_2 \in \mathcal{C}_2$. Computing optimal separation (maximum margin) is a linear or quadratic program depending on norm choice.

6. Extremal Volume Ellipsoids

The maximum volume inscribed ellipsoid (MVIE) and MVEE are dual problems. For a polyhedron, MVIE maximizes $\log \det(A)$ subject to $\|A x_i\|_2 + b^\top x_i \leq 1$, providing inner approximation complementary to MVEE.

7. Placement and Location Problems

Optimal facility location minimizes worst-case or average distance to demand points: $\min_x \max_i \|x - x_i\|$ (Chebyshev) or $\min_x \sum_i w_i\|x - x_i\|$ (Weber). For $\ell_2$ norm, these are convex SOCP or smooth optimization problems.

8. Shape Fitting and Geometric Approximation

Fitting geometric shapes (lines, planes, circles) to data via least squares: $\min \sum_i d(x_i, \mathcal{S})^2$ where $\mathcal{S}$ is the shape. For linear manifolds this is PCA/SVD; for nonlinear shapes, convex relaxations or alternating minimization apply.

9. Floor Planning and Geometric Design

Optimal layout of rectangles (e.g., VLSI design) under area/aspect constraints and non-overlap requirements. Convex formulations arise when variables are logarithmic widths/heights, yielding geometric programs.

Exercises

Try these problems to deepen your understanding. Solutions are provided at the end of the course.

  1. Show that projection onto a convex set $\mathcal{C}$ is a non-expansive operator: $\|\text{proj}_\mathcal{C}(x) - \text{proj}_\mathcal{C}(y)\|_2 \leq \|x - y\|_2$.
  2. Prove that for a polyhedron $\{x : Ax \leq b\}$, the Chebyshev center problem $\max_{x_c, r} r$ subject to $a_i^\top x_c + r\|a_i\|_2 \leq b_i$ is a linear program.
  3. For the MVEE problem, show that the objective $\log \det(A^{-1})$ is convex by verifying that the Hessian is positive semidefinite.
  4. Derive the dual of the MVEE problem and show its connection to the maximum volume inscribed ellipsoid (MVIE).
  5. Show that the distance between two convex sets $\mathcal{C}_1, \mathcal{C}_2$ equals zero if and only if their intersection is nonempty.
  6. For separating hyperplane problem, prove that if two convex sets are disjoint, there exists a hyperplane strictly separating them.
  7. Formulate the Weber problem (minimize sum of weighted distances $\sum_i w_i \|x - x_i\|_2$) as an SOCP.
  8. Show that projection onto the probability simplex $\Delta_n$ can be computed in $O(n \log n)$ time via sorting.
  9. Prove that the smallest enclosing circle of a finite point set has at most 3 points on its boundary (in 2D), and derive an algorithm to find it.
  10. For floor planning with $n$ rectangles, explain how changing variables to logarithms of widths/heights transforms the problem into a geometric program.
